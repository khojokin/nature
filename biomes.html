Why Biomes Matter in WildAtlas

Biomes help explain why certain species live where they do. They connect climate, geography, soil, water, and living communities into one clear ecological picture.

By understanding biomes, readers can see how habitats shape adaptation, food webs, biodiversity, and environmental change across the rest of WildAtlas.

Connect climate with life Biomes reveal how temperature, rainfall, and landscape determine what can survive in a place.
Understand habitat structure Each biome creates distinct conditions for species interactions, vegetation, and ecological balance.
Link to wider systems Biomes connect naturally to species, Earth systems, impact, places, and conservation challenges.
