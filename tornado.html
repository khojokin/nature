Tornado Behavior, Power, and Safety

A tornado is a rapidly rotating column of air that extends from a thunderstorm to the ground. Some are thin and short-lived, while others become wide, violent, and capable of causing catastrophic destruction.

Tornadoes can change direction, strengthen quickly, and produce flying debris that makes them especially dangerous. Even weaker tornadoes can damage roofs, trees, vehicles, and power lines.

The safest response is usually to move to a strong interior shelter on the lowest level of a building, away from windows, and stay alert to weather warnings during severe storm conditions.
