Bird Habits, Eggs, and Natural Role

Birds are warm-blooded animals with feathers, wings, beaks, and strong senses. They spend much of their time searching for food, caring for nests, avoiding predators, and moving across landscapes through flight or ground movement.

A female bird lays eggs in a nest, and many bird species incubate the eggs carefully until chicks hatch. Parents often feed and protect young birds until they are strong enough to fly or survive on their own.

Birds play an important role in ecosystems by spreading seeds, controlling insect populations, pollinating some plants, and serving as indicators of environmental health across many habitats.
