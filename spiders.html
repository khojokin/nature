Spider Behavior, Silk, and Survival

Spiders are arachnids, not insects, and they are known for having eight legs, multiple eyes, and silk-producing organs. Their silk can be used for webs, egg sacs, shelter, climbing, and escaping danger.

Some spiders wait patiently in webs, while others actively hunt prey. Different species use different survival strategies, but many rely on vibration sensing, camouflage, speed, and venom to capture food.

Although spiders are often feared, most are harmless to humans and are extremely useful in ecosystems because they help keep insect populations under control.
