Jaguar Habits, Cubs, and Natural Role

Jaguars are large wild cats recognized by their golden coats and dark rosette markings. They are powerful hunters and are especially known for their strong jaws and ability to take down a wide range of prey.

A female jaguar usually gives birth to a small number of cubs and raises them in hidden shelter. The cubs stay close to their mother while learning to move, hide, and eventually hunt on their own.

As top predators, jaguars help keep ecosystems balanced by controlling prey populations. Protecting jaguars also helps protect forests, wetlands, and many other species that share the same habitat.
