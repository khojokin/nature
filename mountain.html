Mount Everest — The Roof of the World

Mount Everest is the highest mountain on Earth above sea level, rising dramatically in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and China. Its snow-covered summit has fascinated explorers, climbers, and scientists for generations. Towering above surrounding peaks, Everest symbolizes endurance, ambition, and the raw power of nature.

The mountain is part of the Himalayan range, which formed when the Indian tectonic plate collided with the Eurasian plate millions of years ago. This ongoing geological movement still pushes the mountains higher over time.

Despite its beauty, Everest presents extreme conditions including freezing temperatures, powerful winds, and thin air at high altitude. Only well-prepared climbers with careful support can attempt the ascent safely.

Mountains like Everest are not just landmarks; they influence weather, provide water to millions of people through glacier systems, and support unique ecosystems adapted to high elevations.

Across cultures, great mountains have long been seen as sacred, mysterious, and inspiring places. Everest remains one of the world's most iconic natural wonders.
