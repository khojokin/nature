Tiger Habits, Cubs, and Natural Role

Tigers are among the largest wild cats in the world and are known for their orange coats with black stripes. These stripes help break up their outline in forests and tall grass, making them harder for prey to spot.

A female tiger usually gives birth to a small litter of cubs, and the cubs stay with their mother while learning how to hide, move, and eventually hunt. Early survival can be difficult, which makes maternal care extremely important.

As top predators, tigers help regulate prey populations and support ecosystem balance. Protecting tigers often also means protecting large natural landscapes filled with many other species.
